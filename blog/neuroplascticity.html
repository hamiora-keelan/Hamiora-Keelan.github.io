Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neurons create a complex neural network throughout the body's nervous system, allowing cells to form connections and communicate with each other, these connections are called synapses. Neuroplasticity refers to the ability of synapses to form new connections and grow stronger through experiences such as learning and practicing skills. Neuroplasticity also plays a big part in memory and retention through the forming of new connections, try this activity taken from Kevin Horsley's instantly recalling activity Ted Talk and skip to 8:05 to watch your brain form new connections real time.

The benefits of understanding neuroplasticity

Understanding and believing in the principles of neuroplasticity is beneficial as it:

  • Positions you to readily accept a growth mindset through understanding your capabilities are not determined at birth and you are in control of your learning.
  • It allows you to tailor your learning journey and learn new techniques that will optimise your learning.

Actions I can take to benefit from the principles of neuroplasticity

Whilst understanding neuroplasticity primes you for learning, there are further steps you can take to create new connections and strengthen exisiting connections.

  • Pick up a creative hobby: creativity forces the brain to think in new ways through exploring different perspectives. This creates flexibility in your neural network, and strengthens your ability to form connections between previously unrelated subjects.
  • Learning a new skill: Learning new skills enhances neuroplasticity by forming fresh neural connections and reinforcing existing ones. It engages diverse brain regions, fostering synaptic strength and adaptability. Through practice, challenges, and problem-solving, it promotes resilience and cognitive flexibility. This lifelong learning sustains mental agility, reducing the risk of cognitive decline.
  • Engaging your senses: When you actively use your senses—sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell—you activate corresponding brain regions, encouraging the formation of new connections and reinforcing existing ones. This multisensory input enhances synaptic plasticity, the brain's ability to adapt and reorganize in response to sensory experiences. Additionally, sensory engagement promotes cross-modal integration, where information from different senses is combined, leading to more robust and interconnected neural networks. As a result, actively engaging your senses through experiences like exploring new environments, trying new foods, or learning through hands-on activities can significantly enhance neuroplasticity, fostering a more adaptable and resilient brain.
  • Practice Mindfulness: Mindfulness practices enhance neuroplasticity by reshaping brain structure and function. They increase gray matter density in areas related to attention and self-awareness, strengthen connectivity between brain regions, and reduce amygdala activity, fostering emotional resilience. Mindfulness also affects neurotransmitter function, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. Additionally, it may stimulate neurogenesis, supporting the birth of new neurons. Overall, mindfulness fosters adaptive changes in the brain, promoting mental well-being and cognitive flexibility.

Growth Mindset

What does having a growth mindset mean?

My understanding of a growth mindset is to embrace challenges and persevere through obstacles, understanding that talents can also be developed through effort and hard work. This applies to both those who believe they are untalented and those who are talented; as long as you believe there is a ceiling to your growth, your progress will remain hindered at some point.

What was unexpected during this activity?

When I originally began researching the growth mindset, I felt so strongly that I had a growth mindset and I may be wasting time learning it again. However, I quickly realised I was stuck in a paradox; that I was limiting my growth assuming I had no room to grow my growth mindset.

Actions I can take to integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey

  • Embrace feedback: Many individuals offer feedback with positive intentions, aiming to support your growth. Being receptive to input from colleagues, friends, family, and managers can facilitate personal development. However, it's important to maintain a critical mindset, as not all feedback may be transparent or constructive; some might necessitate additional interpretation.
  • Practice Mindfulness: Mindfulness allows individuals to recognize when they are operating from a fixed mindset—the belief that abilities are innate and unchangeable. By identifying these triggers, individuals can consciously shift towards a growth mindset—the belief that abilities can be developed through dedication and effort.
