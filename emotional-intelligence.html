Emotional Intelliigence: Why it's so important

Introduction

Emotional intelligence, the ability to navigate both our own emotions and those of others, is crucial. Every day, we encounter over 400 emotional stimulus that significantly influence our behaviour. Developing emotional intelligence and mindfulness empowers us to manage these emotions and actions, not just for ourselves, but also for those around us.

EQ vs IQ vs personality

Emotional Intelligence (EQ), Intelligence Quotient (IQ), and personality are distinct aspects of human capabilities, each contributing to an individual's overall makeup. IQ represents cognitive abilities, encompassing reasoning, problem-solving, and logical thinking. It is often measured through standardized tests and is considered an indicator of intellectual prowess. On the other hand, EQ revolves around emotional awareness, empathy, and interpersonal skills. It reflects one's ability to recognize, understand, and manage emotions in oneself and others. While IQ is associated with cognitive prowess, EQ emphasizes the social and emotional dimensions of intelligence.

Personality, in contrast, encompasses enduring patterns of thoughts, behaviors, and emotions that shape an individual's responses to various situations. It is not strictly measured like IQ or EQ but is often assessed through personality tests. Personality traits, such as extroversion, conscientiousness, openness, agreeableness, and neuroticism (the Big Five), provide insights into how individuals typically approach life and interact with others. While EQ focuses on emotional acumen, IQ on cognitive abilities, personality delves into the stable and consistent behavioral tendencies that define an individual's uniqueness. In essence, these three dimensions—EQ, IQ, and personality—contribute in tandem to the rich tapestry of human complexity.

EQ vs IQ vs Personality

Developing emotional intelligence involves cultivating two primary competencies, each encompassing both internal and external dimensions.

  1. Personal competencies:

    • Internal aspect: Cultivating self-awareness is crucial; it involves recognizing and understanding your own emotions and feelings. A heightened self-awareness enables you to anticipate your emotional responses to stimuli, even those that may occur in the distant future.
    • External aspect: Self-management is the practical application of self-awareness. Combining a deep understanding of your emotions with effective self-management empowers you to regulate your behaviors in response to incoming emotional stimuli.

  2. Social competencies:

    • Internal aspect: Nurturing social awareness is essential for understanding the emotions of those around you. This skill develops through active listening and attentiveness to both auditory and visual cues from others. A heightened social awareness provides you with the ability to anticipate the emotions of those in your social sphere.
    • External aspect: Relationship management involves applying emotional intelligence to navigate and influence the emotions of those around you. It goes beyond understanding emotions to actively shaping and fostering positive connections with others. Effective relationship management is the outward expression of emotional intelligence in social contexts.

Mindfulness

This is not a rejection of growth; instead, it underscores the importance of remaining mindful during the growth process. It involves the ability to discern what makes you unique and what aspects may require change.
